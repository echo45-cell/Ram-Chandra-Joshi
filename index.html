About Me

A journey shaped by hardship, discipline, and hope.

I was born and raised in a rural village where educational resources were limited, and every step toward learning demanded courage. My school was far from home, books were scarce, electricity was unreliable, and opportunities often felt out of reach. There were many moments when these challenges could have become reasons to give up.

Instead, I chose determination. I studied with discipline, valued every page I had the chance to read, and believed that education had the power to transform lives. Despite long walks, difficult circumstances, and limited facilities, I continued learning with patience, perseverance, and faith in a better future.

That journey shaped me into the teacher I am today. As a +2 level teacher at Shajendraswar Secondary school, I bring the lessons of my own struggles into every classroom. I strive to guide my students with empathy, responsibility, and dedication, encouraging them to believe in themselves, overcome obstacles, and realize their full potential through the power of education.

Teaching Philosophy

Teaching with purpose, patience, and possibility.

Education builds society by preparing thoughtful, capable, and responsible citizens. Every student deserves guidance that respects their individuality and helps them grow with confidence. Learning should inspire curiosity, encourage questions, and connect knowledge with real life. A teacher does more than explain lessons; a teacher shapes future generations through values, discipline, compassion, and belief in each learner's potential.
